## How Google ranks local search (so you know what we're optimising)

When someone searches "hairdressers droitwich", Google shows two things:

1. **The Local Map Pack** (top 3 businesses with map, reviews, hours) — driven by your **Google Business Profile**
2. **Organic blue links** below it — driven by your **website**

Ranking factors, by weight (2026 data):
- **Proximity** to the searcher — 55% of local pack ranking variance
- **Google Business Profile** signals (category, completeness) — 32%
- **Reviews** (rating + recency + volume) — ~20%
- **On-page local SEO** (service pages, NAP, local keywords)
- **Citations** (consistent listings on directories)
- **Backlinks** from local sites

The good news: **you already lead on reviews** (5.0★, 297 Fresha + 62 Google). We build the strategy around that strength.

### PART 2 — Reviews: your unfair advantage 🟢 STRENGTH — PROTECT & EXTEND

You have 5.0★ and 297 reviews. But Google's local pack only counts **Google reviews** for ranking (62) — Fresha reviews don't directly feed Google's algorithm.

**The 18-day rule:** rankings cliff if you go 3 weeks without a new Google review. Velocity matters more than total.

- [ ] **Get every client to leave a GOOGLE review** (not just Fresha). Aim for **5–8 new Google reviews/month minimum**.
  - Create a short link / QR code that opens your Google review form directly. Put it on:
    - The card you hand clients at checkout
    - A small sign at the till / mirror stations
    - Your Fresha automated follow-up message
    - Instagram bio + Stories
- [ ] **Respond to every review** within 48h (88% of consumers favour businesses that respond). Mention the service + Droitwich naturally in replies ("So glad you love your balayage — see you next time in Droitwich!").
- [ ] **Never gate reviews** (don't pre-screen happy vs unhappy — it's banned by Google and the FTC).

> Getting Google reviews from 62 → 150+ over the next 6 months, with steady velocity, will move you up the pack more than almost anything else.
